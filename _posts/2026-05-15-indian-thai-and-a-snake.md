**“We say in Thailand- If you encounter a snake and an Indian , kill the Indian first”**
 …haiyyyyaaaaaa.. who would have thought that this random staggering discovery on an equally random Reddit thread would actually lead to an inadvertent awakening ?  

For starters, why, what and who (***okay I know about the infamous tourists but I'm turning a blind eye to them for now..don’t want to go down that rabbit hole ***) made the Thais be so mad at us .. I dare not step out with a Thai person alone - what if that god forsaken snake shows up? Well , I know who’s going to get the stick  

And then came all the frenzied reading on the history, origins of the cultures.. shared and exclusive alike .. trust me nothing fuels the spirit more than an intent out of spite  

While I never really managed  to climb up the ladders to get to the snakes but I did get reacquainted with Buddhism - the common thread binding these two nations, but then this time in a completely different light  

Documentaries fascinate me , the good ones I admit are rare to find but if you do , they leave you more conversant or ample enough to brag to your ignorant friends  

And then I stumbled upon this exceptional documentary about Buddha produced by PBS. It follows the life of prince Siddhartha to Gautama  Buddha (***the enlightened one ***). Backed by a striking narration, the most engaging background score and vibrant visuals, this documentary captivates all your senses - a true piece of art in itself I must say.  

While Buddhism endorses a lot of beliefs and concepts , what struck me the most were the obvious (***yet oblivious ***) ones - living in the present and not glorifying suffering .. and the fact that they perfectly complement each other if you actually let them sink in- this idea of having an exquisitely perfect future, sometimes at the expense of subjecting yourself to constant worrying and stress, for a future as ambiguous as Kim Kardashian’s pursuit of a career in the law.  

While suffering comes in all shapes and flavors - it could be as small as letting this classic French dessert sitting at the back of your fridge for a whole week, waiting for the perfect Friday evening to relish it ..

Every time you open the fridge door, you force yourself to look away (***in disappointment ***), fighting the urge to savor it and obviously steering clear the endless possibilities of joy that dessert could bring you.
And all this only for you to be ambushed seeing  it spoilt on that designated evening...not worth it right ?


Anyway, it's just the after effects of the documentary I would be going back my old ways soon.. but suffering is indeed subjective and so is living in the present, people with a penchant for bdsm sure have their own arguments... the key is while you cannot really escape suffering, justifying it for a fictional future is not really the answer (***The people from the documentary quoted it on behalf of the Buddha, not me ***) 

A Brahmin once saw the Buddha meditating at the foot of a tree - graceful and peaceful. The Buddha reminded him of the tusker elephant with controlled faculties. He then approached the Buddha and asked him  

**"Who are you?"**

>Imagine me as the lotus flower that started underwater but grew and rose above the surface until it stood free,
>  
>I too have transcended the world and attained the Supreme  
>  
>"Remember me as the one who woke up",  
>The Buddha said
