I often replay what Ethan Hawke said about unrequited love,

>“The one who’s in love always wins
>
>It doesn’t matter if you get your heart broken; you’re living. 
>
> When you’re feeling, you’re alive.
>
>The sun doesn’t care whether the grass appreciates its rays, right? 
>
>It just keeps on shining.
>
>That’s you”


And while <mark> I think it’s beautiful </mark>, empowering to give that love its rightful place - just as any other , in all its beauty, no more and no less, I do find myself on the fence with it. 

<mark>Because love is love, right</mark> ? Who gives anyone the right to undermine it, and for you to love someone having to justify with reasons , would that even be love?

But as my sister often jokes that I came with a long list of disclaimers (*oh I thrive on them *) when I was born , I somehow did manage to find one with this as well.

I remember this conversation from the movie Lion King, one which Mufasa has with a young Simba , 

>“Everything the light touches is our kingdom.”
>
>“What about that shadowy place?”
>
>“That’s beyond our borders”
>
>“We must never go there Simba.”

And that’s precisely how it begins. You step out from the light into the shadows. Yet in those dark clouds, you somehow manage to find your silver lining- <mark>a potential of flame </mark>. 

A <mark>spark</mark>, and don’t we say it always starts with one ?

 You think it does , and before you know it, you are engulfed by those scarlet red hues as a whole, completely mesmerised - spellbound. 


Then again, fire is that warmth, the comfort you would want to seek on a winter’s night. The more cold it gets outside, the more you get drawn to it and the more you want to hold on to it. 

>But wouldn’t courting the flame all by yourself only invite the ashes ?

To me , I don’t find an unrequited love any different from that raging red flame of fire - <mark>bewitching</mark>. And I would rather respect that love by letting it stay afar, and not glorifying it to an extent that I am willing to let it consume me. 

Because the thing is , you are never going to get your closure with a love like this , so it would only be unfair to your own self waiting for an ending to be written - but trust, me the moment you decide to walk away.. it (ending ) will neither be a bad nor a good one, but it will be <mark>YOURS</mark> because you chose yourself, above all else.  

If you have got your hands burnt , you might make sense of this or may be not , and that’s completely fine too - you do you ! 

But for me , I would rather sit with a stick - not to poke the flame but to chase any such potential sparks off to come my way (and also to randomly hit people, because why not ? ) 

Because from now , <mark>the one who loves me back is precisely my type</mark>  

Again, I’m not dismissing it and neither disagreeing ( I should coin a term for agreeing with disclaimers) but there is always a fine line -a grey area if you will and if you still want to be that Simba , tread wisely.

And like Mufasa said,

<mark>Being brave doesn't mean you go looking for trouble</mark>  

On a serious note,

> Unrequited love is like Mumbai’s traffic- driven, vibrant and always the one to promiseyou thrills.  
> 
>And you know you are brave , for someone to drive in the Mumbai traffic and from someone who has done it - I give it to you for that.
>
> But then you should know the exact moment to hit the brakes, else you wouldn’t know which weirdo might hit you hard !


***Happy Friday !***
