***I was told of a huge wooden door***  
***hiding serpents coiled like veils,***  
***an ancient lore***  

***Don’t go near***  
***said a voice trembling in fear***  

***I heard one say they are deadly,***  
***Mirrors of illusion,***  
***left to grapple with beastly***  

***You might hear them laugh on a fine day***  
***making jolly,***  
***tender breathings at play***  


***Don’t open***  
***For you would only hear the words***  
***unspoken***  

***Of regrets, the almosts and the what ifs***  
***The life once dreamt,***  
***now slipping into a drift***  

***Don’t we all hold this door shut close ?***  
***Only to be betrayed by the shadows***  
***bleeding through cracks,***  
***that the light unfroze***  


Imagine waking up one day and finding out you can poetry too ... new fear unlocked.


Anyhoo, I  remembered this article I once read about the hidden doors in the  Sri Padmanabhaswamy Temple (*Kerala, India*) and the myths surrounding the one vault which has been shut indefinitely due to the fear of serpents . I could have treated this as just another news article and went about my day but **NO** -  I couldn’t help but think of these as metaphors to the baggage we carry , our own Pandora’s box - the one we guard quite well.  
  
  

>A Pandora's box
>
>
>A box of one half of a hello,the silent goodbyes,  
> of roads never taken and dreams left uncharted.
>
>
>The unsettling unwavering tension caught between to open this door or let it be shut just as those many things from the past.
>
>
>To be honest , it’s daunting - for we always are going to find comfort in things familiar, and then the past being the only intimate domestic thing you could have known,   
>you probably like the familiarity of it.
>
>
>Anyway, while the present will always be the uncomfortable truth we don’t want to seek ,
> it actually is another story in writing which we still can fix :))
